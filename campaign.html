Campaign Proposal

50 Years Strong,
50 Years Forward

An integrated marketing campaign that converts anniversary momentum into long-term ridership growth, brand loyalty, and community investment in transit's future.

The Campaign Theme

"50 Years Strong, 50 Years Forward"

SamTrans has been the connective tissue of San Mateo County for half a century. Now, as we invest in the next 50 years — new vehicles, new routes, new technologies, a greener grid — we invite the community to be part of that future. Every ride is a vote for the county we want to become.

Strategic Objectives

What This Campaign Must Accomplish

  • Convert anniversary awareness into trial ridership — move casual supporters to regular riders
  • Establish SamTrans as a forward-looking innovator — not just a legacy institution, but a mobility leader
  • Build a rider community with an identity — people who are proud to say they ride SamTrans
  • Drive employer and institutional partnerships — framing commute mode-shift as a business value
  • Create a content engine that sustains engagement year-round — not a burst campaign but an always-on presence
  • Demonstrate marketing ROI — build the measurement framework the agency needs to make the case for marketing investment
Campaign Philosophy

How We'll Win

"Transit agencies don't lose on quality — they lose on story. People know BART. They don't know SamTrans. This campaign makes SamTrans a brand people identify with, root for, and choose."

The campaign is built on three truths: (1) History earns trust. (2) Future earns excitement. (3) Community earns loyalty. Every asset, channel, and activation reflects at least one of these.

Audience Strategy

Who We're Talking To — and How

Different audiences require different messages, channels, and calls to action. This campaign segments intentionally rather than broadcasting generically.

👩‍💻

Young Professionals & Students

Ages 18–34 · Primary Growth Segment

New to the workforce or the Peninsula. Transit-curious, sustainability-motivated, cost-conscious. They'll become lifelong riders if we meet them now.

Message: "Your commute does more than get you to work. It shapes the region you live in."

Channels: Instagram Reels, TikTok, campus activations, streaming audio
💼

Peninsula Commuters

Ages 30–55 · Ridership Recovery Segment

Drove pre-pandemic, returned post-COVID, open to transit again if the reliability story is compelling. Employer pass programs are a key conversion lever.

Message: "Your employer saves when you ride. So does the planet. So does your commute."

Channels: LinkedIn, email, employer partnership programs, out-of-home near stations
🏭

Civic & Business Leaders

Decision-Makers · Advocacy Segment

City council members, chamber leaders, developers, employers. They shape policy, allocate parking, and set commute culture. SamTrans needs them as amplifiers and advocates.

Message: "The companies and cities that invest in transit infrastructure will attract and retain talent for the next decade."

Channels: Events, executive briefings, earned media, LinkedIn
👪

Essential Workers & Dependent Riders

Core Riders · Retention & Advocacy Segment

The backbone of SamTrans ridership. They ride because they need to — but they deserve to feel like they matter, not like an afterthought. Their stories are the campaign's most powerful content.

Message: "You've been with us for 50 years. We're building the next 50 around you."

Channels: On-vehicle content, community events, Spanish-language outreach, Facebook
⚡

Sustainability-Motivated Riders

All Ages · Values-Based Segment

Climate-conscious San Mateo County residents who choose or want to choose transit as part of a low-carbon lifestyle. Electric bus fleet is a major hook.

Message: "Every SamTrans ride removes a car from the road. Our electric fleet means your commute is cleaner than ever."

Channels: Digital display, environmental partnerships, NextDoor, earned media
💡

The Unconverted

Non-Riders · Awareness & Trial Segment

People who drive by default and haven't thought seriously about transit. They need awareness, then an easy on-ramp trial (free ride promotion, app promo, route finder).

Message: "You might be surprised how easy it is. Try it free."

Channels: Paid social, search, geotargeted display, OOH, radio
Message Architecture

Three Pillars, One Voice

All campaign content ladders up to one of three message pillars. Every asset, post, and activation is accountable to a pillar — no off-strategy content.

Pillar 01

Rooted in This Community

We've been here through every chapter of San Mateo County's story. We know these roads, these neighborhoods, these people.

  • Rider story series featuring real passengers
  • Historical archive content — 50 years of routes
  • Community milestone map (births, commutes, graduations SamTrans made possible)
  • Driver and operator spotlights
Pillar 02

Built for What's Next

We're not standing still. New electric buses, improved frequency, real-time tech, and expanded access are reshaping what transit looks like in the county.

  • Electric fleet reveal campaign
  • Future route and investment announcements
  • "What's Next" video series with leadership
  • Data visualization: your county by transit
Pillar 03

Better Together

Every rider is part of something bigger — a county that moves together, reduces congestion, and builds a more equitable, livable region for everyone.

  • Employer commuter challenge campaigns
  • Environmental impact calculator ("your rides = X cars off road")
  • Co-branded city/town campaigns
  • "Forward Together" community pledge

Campaign Timeline

Four Phases, One Arc

The campaign is structured as a year-long arc — not a burst and rest, but a sustained drumbeat with seasonal peaks tied to commuter behavior and SamTrans milestones.

1

Launch — "The Next Chapter Begins"

Months 1–2 · Campaign kickoff and brand platform launch
  • Campaign brand launch — new visual identity extension from 50th anniversary palette
  • Full campaign TVC / video launch across streaming and social
  • Press release and media briefing with CEO and Board Chair
  • Employer partner program launch — 10 target companies in month 1
  • Social content series kicks off: "50 Riders, 50 Stories" (one per week, 50 weeks)
  • Free trial ride promotion — app download + route discovery callout
2

Build — "Community in Motion"

Months 3–6 · Deepening and widening reach
  • Electric fleet reveal event — media day, social live coverage, rider celebration
  • Youth rider activation: campus partnerships, student transit pass promotion
  • "Better Together" commuter challenge — employer teams compete on transit days
  • Spanish-language campaign expansion across all primary channels
  • Civic leader briefing series — quarterly lunches with municipal partners
  • Rider story campaign peaks — major production push, long-form video features
3

Peak — "The Forward Push"

Months 7–9 · Maximum investment, fall commuter season
  • Back-to-work / back-to-school media blitz — highest paid media spend of the year
  • Annual SamTrans Community Summit — riders, advocates, partners, and media
  • Out-of-home takeover campaign in Peninsula job corridors
  • Commuter habit challenge: "30 Days on SamTrans" — social documentary series
  • Mid-year campaign performance review — adjust channel mix based on data
  • Partnership announcement push — new employer, city, or nonprofit co-brands
4

Sustain — "Always Moving"

Months 10–12 · Year-round momentum and next-year planning
  • Holiday and year-end campaign — gratitude, community, celebration
  • Annual Impact Report launch — marketing storytelling around ridership data
  • Campaign audit and results briefing with leadership
  • Next-year planning kickoff — community insights, rider feedback, channel performance
  • "Rider of the Year" recognition — community engagement and press moment
  • Refresh content series — begin year-2 pilot content and testing
