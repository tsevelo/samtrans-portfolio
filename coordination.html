Program Management

The Coordination Reality

Great campaigns don't just need great ideas — they need rigorous systems. This section covers how I'd manage the agencies, vendors, approvals, timelines, and stakeholders that make integrated marketing actually work.

📋 My Program Management Philosophy

The PM's job isn't to control everything — it's to make sure everyone else can do their best work. That means: clear briefs so vendors don't guess, clean approval processes so reviews don't stall, and proactive communication so nothing is a surprise. I run campaigns like a conductor, not a gatekeeper.

How Work Gets Done

Campaign Intake & Production Workflow

1

Strategic Brief

Every project starts with a written brief: objective, audience, key message, channels, timeline, and success metrics. No brief = no kickoff. I write or co-write every brief with the requesting stakeholder.

Brief Template 1-page format 24-hr turnaround SLA
2

Scope & Timeline Lock

Once the brief is approved, I scope the work with agencies and create a production timeline with hard milestones. All deadlines are back-calculated from the live date, not front-loaded from kickoff.

Asana / Monday.com Gantt view Buffer days built in
3

Agency Kickoff

Brief walk-through with creative and media teams. Questions answered in the room, not via endless email. I document all decisions and open items before the call ends.

Action item tracker Shared Slack channel
4

Review Cycles (Structured)

Maximum two rounds of internal revision before work goes to leadership for approval. I consolidate feedback before returning to agencies — no round-robin conflicting notes. Review windows are scheduled, not open-ended.

Frame.io / Bynder 48-hr review windows
5

Final Approval & Launch Checklist

Nothing goes live without a signed-off approval and a completed launch checklist: all assets reviewed, all tracking set up, all partners notified, all team members briefed on timing.

Launch checklist template Go/No-Go meeting
6

In-Flight Monitoring & Reporting

Weekly performance pulls from all channels. Monthly campaign report to leadership — one page, numbers + narrative. If something is underperforming, I flag it in week 2, not week 8.

Google Looker Studio Weekly dashboard Anomaly alerts

Real-World Scenarios

How I Handle Hard Situations

Every campaign hits turbulence. Here's how I'd respond to common high-stakes scenarios in this role.

🚨

Scenario: The Agency Misses a Major Deadline

The creative agency was supposed to deliver hero video assets two weeks before launch. They're now 5 days out and still not done.

My Response

  • Call the agency account lead immediately — get a realistic delivery date, not a hopeful one
  • Assess what can launch on time vs. what needs to be delayed or swapped for a simpler asset
  • Brief my CMO same day: here's the situation, here's my recommendation, here's what I need from you (if anything)
  • Explore contingencies: can we launch with still images while video finalizes? Can media buy be delayed 3 days?
  • Document the miss formally and schedule a post-mortem to fix the process — then hold the agency to the written correction
⚙

Scenario: Internal Stakeholders Send Conflicting Feedback

Legal wants changes to copy. Government Affairs wants a different message priority. The CMO wants the original direction. The agency is waiting.

My Response

  • Pause the agency review clock — don't let conflicting notes land on their desk
  • Convene a 30-minute internal alignment call with all three stakeholders — not email threads
  • Facilitate the conversation: "We agree on X. The disagreement is Y. Here's the decision we need to make."
  • Escalate to CMO if stakeholders can't resolve — present options with tradeoffs, not just the problem
  • Once decided, send a single consolidated brief back to the agency with one set of notes
📈

Scenario: A Key Campaign Channel Is Underperforming

Six weeks in, paid social is at 40% of projected click-through rate. The media agency shrugs and says "give it more time."

My Response

  • Pull the full channel breakdown — is this platform-wide or specific to one audience / creative?
  • Request a formal diagnosis from the media agency: what's their hypothesis? What test will they run?
  • Set a 2-week optimization window with clear success criteria — not open-ended "let's see"
  • If no improvement, recommend reallocating spend to a stronger channel and present the data to CMO
  • Update the measurement framework to capture what we're learning for the next campaign
💲

Scenario: Mid-Year Budget Cut of 20%

Finance comes back with a 20% reduction to the marketing budget. The campaign is already in flight.

My Response

  • Map every active spend line to campaign objectives — identify what drives results vs. what's "nice to have"
  • Build three scenarios: cut low-performing channels, reduce scope of future phases, or pause one audience segment
  • Present to CMO with my recommendation and the tradeoff each option creates
  • Communicate changes to agencies with clear scope adjustments and revised SOWs
  • Update the measurement dashboard to reflect new targets — don't hold the campaign to original KPIs with 20% less resource
Agency Management

How I Run Vendor Relationships

Agencies perform best when the client is organized, decisive, and honest. I'm all three. Here's what that looks like in practice.

Creative Agency

Clear Briefs, Fast Decisions

Agencies waste hours interpreting vague requests. I write tight briefs and convene fast alignment calls. When they present work, I come with consolidated, actionable feedback — not "I'll think about it."

📋 SOW reviews · Monthly scope check-ins · Quarterly performance reviews
Media Buying Agency

Data-First Accountability

I require a structured reporting cadence from day one: what we're measuring, how often, in what format. Media agencies who can't show me weekly performance data on a dashboard get a hard conversation in week 3.

📈 Weekly reporting · Monthly optimization reviews · Quarterly strategic assessments
PR / Comms Agency

Integrated Into the Campaign

PR shouldn't be a silo. I integrate the PR agency into the campaign calendar from day one so they can amplify paid and owned moments with earned media coverage at the right time.

📰 Shared content calendar · Rapid response protocol · Monthly media relationship updates
All Vendors

Fair but Firm Contracts

I know how to read a scope of work, spot scope creep early, and renegotiate when reality diverges from the original SOW. I also know how to recognize when we're the problem — and fix that first.

📋 SOW ownership · Change order tracking · Annual performance reviews
Tools & Systems

My PM Toolkit

📋

Project Management

Asana, Monday.com, or Smartsheet for campaign timelines. Every project has an owner, a deadline, and a status visible to the full team — no status updates via memory.

📊

Analytics & Reporting

Google Analytics 4, Looker Studio, and platform-native dashboards (Meta, LinkedIn, Google Ads). Monthly reports built in Looker Studio — shareable links, no emailing decks.

👤

Asset Management

Bynder or a structured Google Drive architecture with naming conventions and version control. Creative chaos is one of the most preventable PM failures.

💬

Communication

Slack for real-time, email for decisions-of-record. Every external agency gets a shared Slack channel. I respond within the hour during business hours — always.

📷

Creative Review

Frame.io for video review, Google Slides for campaign presentations, and a consolidation step before any feedback goes to agencies. One set of notes, maximum twice.

📦

Budget Management

Excel or Google Sheets budget tracker with commitment tracking, actuals vs. plan, and encumbered-vs-spent visibility. Updated weekly; shared with finance monthly.
